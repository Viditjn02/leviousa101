Economics 101 - Assignment #3

Due: Tomorrow 11:59 PM | Time Remaining: 23h 45m
Question 1:
Explain the concept of market equilibrium and how supply and demand curves interact to determine price. Include an example of how external factors might shift these curves and affect equilibrium.
Your Answer:
Market equilibrium is a fundamental concept in economics where the quantity demanded by consumers equals the quantity supplied by producers at a specific price point. This occurs at the intersection of the demand and supply curves. The demand curve typically slopes downward, reflecting the law of demand - as prices increase, quantity demanded decreases. Conversely, the supply curve usually slopes upward, showing that producers are willing to supply more at higher prices. When these curves intersect, they determine the equilibrium price and quantity. At this point, there is no shortage or surplus in the market, and both consumers and producers are satisfied with the exchange. External factors can shift these curves and create new equilibrium points. For example, if consumer income increases for a normal good, the demand curve shifts rightward, leading to a higher equilibrium price and quantity. Similarly, if production costs decrease due to technological advancement, the supply curve shifts rightward, resulting in a lower equilibrium price but higher quantity. A practical example would be the coffee market during a frost in Brazil (a major coffee producer). The frost would reduce coffee supply, shifting the supply curve leftward. This would result in a higher equilibrium price and lower equilibrium quantity until the market adjusts.
